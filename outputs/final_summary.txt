In a world increasingly driven by technology, we face a profound paradox where we are physically isolated while together, yet feel more connected when interacting through screens. While our devices offer instant answers at our fingertips, research suggests they also make us lazier and increasingly disconnected from real-life interactions. To combat this digital drift, the speaker advocates for a "pro-human" approach by reviving three simple "old habits" of behavior. These habits involve pausing for a second before reaching for our phones, letting ourselves wonder without instantly searching for answers, and asking questions out loud to others. By sharing a personal story about their father's passing, the speaker illustrates that these moments of true presence are what matter most. Ultimately, adults must practice these habits themselves to teach future generations how to be genuinely together in the real world.
